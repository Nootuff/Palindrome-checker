What is a Palindrome?

  • • A palindrome is word, phrase, number or sequence of characters that reads the same backwards as forwards, e.g. "madam" or "nurses run".
  • • Sentence-length palindromes may be written when allowances are made for adjustments to capital letters, punctuation, and word dividers, such as "A man, a plan, a canal, Panama!"
  • • The word itself is derived from the Greek roots palin (πάλιν; "again") and dromos (δρóμος; "way, direction")
  • • According to Guinness World Records, the Finnish 19-letter word saippuakivikauppias (a soapstone vendor), is the world's longest palindromic word in everyday use.
